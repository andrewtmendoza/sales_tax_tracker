# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is the self-hosting owner tracking receipts for personal sales tax records. They use the app in short bursts: capturing receipts from a phone, sometimes offline, then reviewing extracted metadata later on a desktop or larger screen.

## Product Purpose

Sales Tax Tracker helps organize photographed receipts, extract merchant, date, total, and sales tax fields, and maintain a reliable year-to-date sales tax total. Success means receipts are easy to capture at the moment, queued safely when offline, and quick to verify without turning the task into accounting software.

## Brand Personality

Mobile-first chore reducer: practical, lightweight, reassuring. The interface should feel like it removes friction from an annoying task, not like a finance dashboard trying to impress.

## Anti-references

Avoid tax software clutter, heavy accounting dashboards, gamified personal-finance visuals, generic SaaS hero styling, and decorative UI that slows down receipt capture or review.

## Design Principles

1. Capture first: the phone flow should make taking a receipt photo the obvious next action.
2. Preserve trust: offline state, sync progress, extraction errors, and saved changes must be explicit.
3. Review in context: receipt image and editable metadata should stay close enough to compare quickly.
4. Reduce chore weight: keep copy short, controls familiar, and visual noise low.
5. Prefer practical polish: delight should come from speed, clarity, and reliable feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No project-specific accessibility requirements were identified. Default to accessible product UI standards: WCAG AA contrast where practical, visible focus states, keyboard-operable controls, readable touch targets, and reduced-motion-safe interactions.
